The Procession

One clock, four voices, played together. The lyre carries the line (the voice of Sappho & LIMEN); under it the two-tone drum keeps the binary pulse (low 0 · high 1, the Kele code); the dùndún talks in three tones and glides like a voice (ternary, ~1.58 bits a beat, the radix nearest e); and the bàtá answers in sharp fixed strokes — the ciphered drum (ẹ̀nà, a compiler's intermediate code). Mute and unmute the layers; they all run through the ternary echo. Binary, ternary, the cipher, and the lyric line — the whole communication procession, sounding at once.

what's real. the two-tone (binary) contour is the Kele drum code (Carrington via Gleick); the three-tone dùndún is the Yorùbá tension drum (high/mid/low, tracks speech at R≥0.98); the plucked lyre voices the Sapphic / LIMEN line. each instrument's voice is a live Web-Audio synthesis. full standalone instruments: the two-tone drum · the dùndún · the bàtá · the Sapphic carrier.
what's the system. the patterns and the way the three are stacked into one groove is ROOT0's arrangement — a render of the family playing together, not a transcribed traditional ensemble. the ternary echo (3 voices, −/0/+) is ROOT0's. respect to living traditions; this is a study, not a recording.
